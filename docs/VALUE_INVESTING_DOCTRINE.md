# Value Investing Doctrine

This doctrine is the default research lens for V1.

To B customers may override or extend it with institution-specific doctrine, but custom doctrine must not weaken evidence traceability, compliance safety, or value trap checks.

## Preferred Company Traits

### Cash Flow Quality

Prefer companies with:

- Stable operating cash flow.
- Free cash flow that is positive or explainably cyclical.
- Net profit supported by cash conversion.
- Limited dependence on aggressive accruals.

Penalize:

- Net profit growth while operating cash flow deteriorates.
- Persistent cash flow weaker than accounting profit.
- Large unexplained working capital deterioration.

### Dividend Quality

Prefer companies with:

- Stable dividend history.
- Dividend policy supported by free cash flow.
- Reasonable payout ratio.
- Clear capital allocation rationale.

Penalize:

- High dividend not covered by free cash flow.
- Dividend funded mainly by historical cash reserves.
- Dividend used to mask weak growth or asset quality issues.
- Dividend policy that sacrifices necessary reinvestment.

### Balance Sheet Safety

Prefer companies with:

- Manageable leverage.
- Limited short-term debt pressure.
- Sufficient cash reserves.
- Clear debt maturity structure.

Penalize:

- ROE supported mainly by high leverage.
- Heavy short-term debt pressure.
- Weak liquidity.
- Off-balance-sheet or contingent risks, when disclosed.

### Business Model Stability

Prefer companies with:

- Understandable revenue and profit sources.
- Stable demand.
- Clear industry position.
- Reasonable capital intensity.
- Long-term competitive advantages.

Penalize:

- Unclear profit source.
- Long-term demand decline.
- Heavy capex that persistently consumes free cash flow.
- Business model dependent on speculative narratives.

### Profit Quality

Prefer companies with:

- Recurring profit sources.
- Explainable margins and ROE.
- Limited non-recurring gain dependence.

Penalize:

- Profit dependent on non-recurring gains.
- Margin or ROE changes without clear business explanation.
- Earnings quality that conflicts with cash flow.

### Management Capital Allocation

Prefer management that:

- Allocates capital rationally.
- Explains dividends, buybacks, reinvestment, expansion, and M&A with evidence.
- Does not avoid core business questions.

Penalize management that:

- Tells long-term stories without execution evidence.
- Chases hot themes without clear fit.
- Avoids cash flow, debt, or capital allocation questions.

### Margin of Safety

Prefer valuation cases where:

- Downside risk is explicitly analyzed.
- Current valuation does not rely on distant high-growth assumptions.
- Key assumptions can be verified.

Penalize:

- Low valuation caused by core business deterioration.
- Safety claims based only on low PE/PB.
- Valuation conclusions without sensitivity or counter-evidence.

## Mandatory Value Trap Checks

Every memo must check:

- Is high dividend sustainable?
- Is low valuation caused by decline rather than mispricing?
- Is profit quality supported by cash flow?
- Is dividend covered by free cash flow?
- Is ROE driven by leverage?
- Is industry demand structurally declining?
- Are receivables or inventory worsening?
- Is management using narrative or dividend to distract from weakness?
- Does the thesis rely too much on optimistic assumptions?

## Confidence Downgrade Triggers

Downgrade confidence when:

- Key financial fields are missing.
- Source references are weak or absent.
- Evidence conflicts across materials.
- Only sell-side views support the conclusion.
- Management narrative conflicts with financial evidence.
- Value trap checks cannot be completed.
- The conclusion depends on unverified future assumptions.
